Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026 (v1.4)

RepeatRx ("the app") is developed by Barry Rose. This policy explains what data the app collects, how it is used, and how it is protected.

1. Data We Collect

RepeatRx stores the following information that you provide:

  • Patient details: full name, date of birth, NHS number, phone number, email address, and home address (one or more patients)
  • GP practice details per patient: practice name, email address, and phone number
  • Medication names, dosages, types, and frequencies per patient
  • A history of prescription requests sent for each patient
  • SMTP email credentials (host, port, username, password)
  • A PIN code (stored as a one-way hash) and biometric preference

2. How Your Data Is Stored

All data is stored locally on your device only. RepeatRx does not operate any servers, does not send your data to any third-party service, and does not use cloud storage or analytics.

  • Personal details and medication data are stored using Apple's SwiftData framework on your device
  • Email credentials and your PIN hash are stored in the iOS Keychain, which is encrypted by the operating system
  • App preferences are stored in the device's local UserDefaults

3. How Your Data Is Used

Your data is used solely to:

  • Compose and send prescription request emails to each patient's GP practice via your own email account
  • Display a history of requests sent for each patient
  • Populate patient details into email and report content where you have enabled this

RepeatRx does not use your data for advertising, analytics, profiling, or any purpose beyond the above.

4. Email Sending

RepeatRx supports two methods for sending prescription requests:

  • Default Mail App: The app opens your device's default mail app (e.g. Mail, Gmail, Outlook) with the email pre-composed. Sending is handled entirely by that app. RepeatRx has no visibility of whether the email was delivered.
  • SMTP: The app connects directly to your configured SMTP email server using credentials you provide. No email content passes through any RepeatRx infrastructure. You are sending email directly from your own account.

5. Camera and Photo Library

If you use the prescription scanning feature, the app may request access to your camera or photo library. Photos are processed entirely on-device using Apple's Vision framework to extract text. No image or photo data is stored by the app, transmitted to any server, or retained after the scan is complete.

6. Medication Report

You can generate a PDF report of your selected medications. This report is created entirely on-device and shared only via the iOS share sheet at your explicit request (e.g. printed, saved to Files, or emailed). The app does not send or store this report anywhere automatically.

7. NHS Directory Search

When searching for a GP practice, the app makes a request to the publicly available NHS Organisation Data Service (ODS) API at directory.spineservices.nhs.uk. No personal data is sent in these requests — only the search term you enter (practice name or postcode).

8. Biometric Data

If you enable Face ID or Touch ID, authentication is handled entirely by iOS using Apple's LocalAuthentication framework. The app never has access to your biometric data.

9. Data Sharing

RepeatRx does not share your data with any third party. Your data never leaves your device except as part of emails you explicitly choose to send.

10. Data Deletion

You can delete your data at any time from within the app:

  • Patients list → swipe to delete — removes a patient and all their medications and history
  • Settings → Clear Data — delete individual categories of data
  • Settings → Clear Data → Reset Application — removes all data, credentials, and your PIN, returning the app to its initial state
  • Deleting the app from your device removes all locally stored data

11. Children's Privacy

RepeatRx is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.

12. Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised date.
